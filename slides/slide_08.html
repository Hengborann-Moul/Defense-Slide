This thesis commits to answering three research questions, and they are mutually reinforcing. The first question, R-Q-one, is a performance baseline: can an X-G-Boost classifier reach competitive accuracy against deep-learning baselines on the four-class DAiSEE task. The second question, R-Q-two, is an interpretability layer: which facial features are most discriminative, and crucially how do their importances shift across the four affective states — because the features that signal boredom are not necessarily the features that signal frustration. The third question, R-Q-three, is a robustness check: how well does the framework handle the dataset's three structural challenges — class imbalance, inter-subject variability, and the temporal dimension. Each R-Q maps to specific slides in the deck, and we have flagged those slide numbers on each card so the committee can navigate quickly during Q-and-A.
