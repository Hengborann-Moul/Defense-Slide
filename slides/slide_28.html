The ablation table confirms the 150-frame design choice. Performance rises sharply from 30 to 75 frames, continues to improve modestly up to 150, then plateaus or slightly decreases at 200 and 300 frames, additional context adds transition noise. 150 frames, or 10 seconds, is the elbow that balances performance against computational cost. The deployment panel on the right is the proof-of-concept: 2.1 milliseconds per-sample inference time and 15 megabytes combined model size, both negligible compared to deep-learning baselines that weigh hundreds of megabytes. The FaceAura app is a working proof, not just a benchmark, it runs end-to-end at 15 frames per second on commodity CPU, no GPU, no cloud. That is what makes real-time classroom deployment feasible on budget hardware.
