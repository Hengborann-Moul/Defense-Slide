Efficient Attention Classification 17 / 34
Walking left to right: boredom is sustained disengagement — we expect eyeBlink, eyeLookDown, jawOpen, and the engineered boredom indicator at index 72 to dominate, with reduced facial animation, downward gaze drift and a flat expression distribution. Engagement is active attention — eyeWide, browInnerUp, mouthSmile and the engagement indicator at index 73. Confusion is cognitive overload — browDown, eyeSquint, mouthFrown and the confusion indicator at index 70. Frustration is an affective barrier — browInnerUp, mouthPress, jawForward and the frustration indicator at index 71. These sub-groups are pre-defined from the FACS literature and encode our prior about which features should matter. The SHAP analysis will validate most of these expectations and reveal two surprises: mouthSmileRight_median dominates boredom — that is social masking — and noseSneerLeft_max is unique to frustration, the lateralization of negative affect. The cross-state bridge at the bottom is the most theoretically interesting result: boredom indicator velocity standard deviation appears in the top-five SHAP for both confusion and frustration, confirming D'Mello and Graesser's affective-transition theory.
