MediaPipe Face Landmarker gives us 468 landmarks and 52 blendshape coefficients per frame. We compose these into a 78-dimensional vector organized into five semantic groups. The first group is the 52 raw blendshapes, the per-action-unit facial muscle activations across eyebrow, eye, jaw, mouth, nose and cheek. The second is 6-D head pose — pitch, yaw, roll plus 3-D position. The third is 6-D eye gaze, computed from blendshapes. The fourth is a 10-D composite group that we engineer ourselves: regional activity levels, symmetry, and state-specific indicators — this is not raw MediaPipe output. The fifth is a 4-D dynamics group: animation level, expression intensity, active region count, and facial tension. The state-specific sub-groups encode our prior about which features should matter per state — the SHAP analysis on slides 22 to 25 will validate this empirically. Throughput is 30-plus frames per second on a single CPU, roughly 3 milliseconds per frame including MediaPipe inference.
