We chose the face as our modality for three reasons. First, it is non-invasive and passive — there are no wearables, no electrodes, and no consent friction, which matters in a classroom setting. Second, facial expressions are universal across cultures, so a model trained on the international DAiSEE benchmark transfers more cleanly to Cambodian learners than culturally bound audio or posture cues would. Third, the MediaPipe Face Mesh library runs at thirty-plus frames per second on a commodity C-P-U, which is critical for low-resource deployment in schools that cannot afford G-P-U hardware. MediaPipe extracts four hundred sixty-eight facial landmarks per frame, and from those landmarks we engineer a seventy-eight-dimensional per-frame feature vector — blendshapes, head pose, eye gaze, composite, and temporal dynamics. The methodology section will detail exactly how each of those seventy-eight features is constructed.
