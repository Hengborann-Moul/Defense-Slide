This table summarizes nine representative studies on the DAiSEE benchmark between twenty sixteen and 2020-four. The headline observation is that almost every published approach reports accuracy somewhere between sixty-three and sixty-eight percent on the four-class task, regardless of how sophisticated the deep architecture is, ResNet plus temporal networks, three-dimensional DenseNet with self-attention, EfficientNet plus L-S-T-M, all land in that band. The one outlier is Santoni and colleagues in 2020-three, who reached seventy-eight percent by combining OpenFace features with S-M-O-T-E oversampling; that is the closest in spirit to our approach. The gap our thesis targets is therefore not raw accuracy but computational cost and interpretability, every entry in this table requires a deep network trained end-to-end on video frames. We ask the inverse question: how far can a lightweight gradient-boosted tree take us, given the right hand-crafted features?
