01 dataset
DAiSEE is narrow
Only 112 subjects, all Indian university students. Generalization to other cultures, age groups, and learning contexts is unproven. Inter-annotator agreement of 0.581 caps achievable performance.
02 visibility
Face-only modality
Excludes body posture, gestures, screen interaction, and physiology. The weakest state — Confusion (F1 = 0.566) — may benefit most from interaction-based signals like mouse movement and response time.
03 videocam_off
Frontal-face assumption
MediaPipe requires a visible, frontal, sufficiently-resolved face. Real classrooms introduce glare, occlusions, side profiles, and varying camera angles that degrade feature quality beyond what the current handler can recover.

Three open challenges we acknowledge. First, DAiSEE is narrow — 112 subjects from a single cultural context, with inter-annotator agreement of only 0.581, which caps achievable performance regardless of model. Second, the framework uses face only — no body posture, no screen interaction, no physiology — and the weakest state, Confusion, may benefit most from interaction-based signals. Third, MediaPipe assumes a frontal, visible face, but real classrooms introduce glare, occlusions, and varying camera angles. These are honest constraints that bound the claims of this thesis, and the next slide addresses each with a concrete future direction.
