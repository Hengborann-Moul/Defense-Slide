This convergence table is the heart of the interpretability story. Three patterns emerge. First, mouthSmileRight_median is transdiagnostic, it appears in the top five for Boredom, Confusion, and Frustration, but with different SHAP directions: a high smile pushes the prediction toward Boredom and away from Confusion. Second, boredom_indicator_vel_std is a cross-state bridge, it tops Confusion and ranks second for Frustration, validating D'Mello and Graesser's affective-transition theory that these emotions share attentional mechanisms. Third, three features are unambiguously state-specific: eye_activity_max is unique to Engagement, noseSneerLeft_max is unique to Frustration, and head_roll_vel_max_abs is unique to Boredom. These state-specific signatures are the most actionable findings for e-learning intervention design, they support dimensional models of affect and let us target interventions precisely.
