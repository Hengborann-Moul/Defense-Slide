Confusion is the hardest state to classify, and SHAP explains why: the top SHAP value is only 0.07 — five times weaker than Boredom's 0.17. The discriminative signal is distributed across many weak features rather than concentrated in one. The top feature, boredom_indicator_vel_std, is a cross-emotion composite, theoretically linking Confusion to Boredom via D'Mello and Graesser's attentional-control theory — both states involve attentional disruption. The eye-gaze feature eyeLookDownLeft_kurtosis captures rare intense downward glances that signal information-seeking. Because no single feature dominates, the classifier defaults to predicting Low for ninety-eight percent of confusion samples. This is precisely why Confusion may benefit most from multimodal features — mouse, scroll, response time — in future work.
