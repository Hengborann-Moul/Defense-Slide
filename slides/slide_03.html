With the agenda in mind, let me now turn to the first section, the Introduction. Over the next seven slides I will lay out the motivation for this work, beginning with the disruption Cambodian education faced during the pandemic. I will then introduce the four affective states that frame the attention-classification problem, summarize a decade of prior work on the DAiSEE benchmark, justify our choice of facial modality and the MediaPipe feature extractor, and finally state the three research questions and the three concrete objectives this thesis commits to answering. The Introduction exists to make the rest of the defense legible, every methodological choice and every empirical result traces back to one of the questions or objectives introduced here.
