Efficient Attention Classification
31 / 34
Three claims the empirical evidence supports. First, small models compete — the lightweight XGBoost at 65.26 percent accuracy outperforms the median deep-learning baseline at 63.9 percent on DAiSEE, at a fraction of the compute cost, running in 2.1 milliseconds per sample. Second, each affective state has its own feature fingerprint — Boredom is blendshapes, Engagement is composite plus gaze, Confusion is composite plus head orientation, Frustration is mouth and jaw blendshapes plus bursting dynamics. No single feature group carries all four states. Third, imbalance is the bottleneck — Frustration Medium and High recall collapses to zero, showing that SMOTE plus class weighting plus Focal Loss is insufficient for the most extreme imbalance. The one-sentence summary captures the whole thesis.
