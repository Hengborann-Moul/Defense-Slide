This is RQ1, does our lightweight XGBoost model compete with deep-learning baselines on DAiSEE? The bar chart shows sixteen methods sorted ascending by 4-class accuracy. The proposed model, highlighted in teal, lands at 65.26 percent, above the deep-learning median of 63.9 percent, a 1.36 percentage-point margin, with a model that fits in under one megabyte versus 100-plus megabytes for the deep models. I want to acknowledge Santoni et al. at 77.97 percent as the current SOTA, but note that it uses SMOTE on OpenFace features, which is essentially our recipe with deep features instead of MediaPipe blendshapes. The takeaway: a lightweight gradient-boosted classifier matches or exceeds the median deep-learning baseline on the same DAiSEE 4-class task, at a fraction of the compute cost.
